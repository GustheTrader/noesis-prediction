## What It Is

A self-hosted, sovereign prediction market where collective members make predictions, track accuracy, and sharpen their signal through the wisdom of crowds.

Not Polymarket. Not Kalshi. **Ours.**

## Why NOESIS

**Noesis** = direct intuitive knowing.

Prediction markets aren't gambling. They're **collective intelligence aggregation.** When smart people put skin in the game, their predictions become signal. The crowd reveals what it actually believes, not what it says it believes.

For the Wrong Room Collective, prediction markets serve three purposes:

1. **Signal sharpening** — Members calibrate their intuition against outcomes
2. **Collective intelligence** — The group's aggregated predictions > any individual
3. **Accountability loop** — Predictions create track records. Track records create accountability.

## Market Types

### 1. Reality OS Markets
Predictions about consciousness, technology, and human evolution:
- "Will AI agents achieve human-level reasoning by 2028?"
- "Will the Wrong Room Collective reach 1000 members by 2027?"
- "Will sovereign AI platforms outnumber corporate ones by 2030?"

### 2. Macro Markets
Big picture predictions:
- "Will the US enter recession by Q4 2026?"
- "Will Bitcoin exceed $200K by 2027?"
- "Will a new political party gain 5%+ in 2028 election?"

### 3. Personal Markets
Track your own predictions:
- "Will I complete X project by date Y?"
- "Will I maintain my morning routine for 30 days?"
- "Will I achieve Z health metric by date?"

### 4. Wrong Room Markets
Collective challenges:
- "Which member will have the highest accuracy this quarter?"
- "What's the most surprising outcome this month?"
- "What domain should the collective explore next?"

## The Scoring System

### Accuracy Score
```
Score = (Prediction Confidence × Outcome) - (Overconfidence Penalty)

Perfect calibration: Score = 1.0
Overconfident: Score < 1.0 (penalized)
Underconfident: Score < 1.0 (missed upside)
```

### Reputation System
- Track record over time
- Domain expertise (do you predict tech better than politics?)
- Calibration (are you well-calibrated or overconfident?)
- Improvement rate (are you getting better?)

### Leaderboard
- Top predictors by domain
- Most improved
- Best calibrated
- "Wrong Room Award" — best predictions that went against the crowd
